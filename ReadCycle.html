ReadCycle: Turning the Page on Textbook Affordability

  • Objective: Understand and solve the challenge of high textbook costs by interviewing students and crafting a user-friendly online marketplace for students to buy, sell, and swap textbooks affordably.
  • Process:
    1. Conducted interviews with Boston College students to understand their struggles with textbook expenses.
    2. Analyzed collected data to identify key user needs and pain points.
    3. Created a low-fidelity prototype using Figma to visualize the initial design concepts.
    4. Designed and developed the high-fidelity ReadCycle platform using HTML, JavaScript, and CSS.
    5. Iteratively tested the platform to ensure it effectively addressed the identified problems.
  • User Insights: Students struggle with the high costs of textbooks, find the current buying and selling process cumbersome, and desire a more affordable and community-focused solution.
  • Project Insight: Simplifying the textbook exchange process and creating a sense of community can significantly reduce the financial burden on students.
  • Outcome: Developed ReadCycle, a low-cost, user-friendly online marketplace that saves students money and simplifies the textbook exchange process while building community on college campuses.
  • Skills Demonstrated: Team Collaboration, User Research, Data Analysis, Prototyping (Figma), Web Development (HTML, JavaScript, CSS), Problem-Solving, Design Thinking
