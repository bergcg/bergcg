Design and Redesign

  • Objective: The first part involved redesigning Florence Nightingale's Coxcomb chart to make it more readable for modern viewers. The second part focused on creating a static visualization using a dataset containing Boston College's enrollment by school and majors since 1990, highlighting key findings.
  • Process:
    1. Redesigned the Coxcomb chart, originally published in 1858, to improve readability and clarity for contemporary audiences. Analyzed the original chart's shortcomings and recreated it as a stacked bar chart with labels, legends, and distinct colors to enhance data representation.
    2. Designed a sunburst chart using a dataset of Boston College's enrollment by school and majors, showcasing the breakdown of enrollment per school and the top 3 majors by percentage. Leveraged glitch to create a website showcasing the design and redesign visuals, providing insights into the process.
  • Findings: The project provided insights into redesigning historical visualizations for modern audiences and creating effective static visualizations to highlight key findings from large datasets. Through this project, I practiced the crucial skill of conveying insights to users in a compelling and accessible manner, emphasizing the importance of effective communication in data interpretation.
  • Skills Demonstrated: Data Analysis, Visualization Design (Excel), Web Development (Glitch), Creative Problem-Solving, Effective Communication.
